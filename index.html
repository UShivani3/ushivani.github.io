About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Waterloo, working under the supervision of Prof. Jimmy Lin. My research specializes in Information Retrieval, Large Language Models, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems.

I focus on developing robust evaluation methodologies for LLMs, enhancing retrieval augmentation techniques, and building automated assessment frameworks for information access systems. My recent work explores multimodal evaluation and scalable benchmarks to better understand LLM behavior in diverse, real-world scenarios.

I completed my Bachelor of Technology (BTech) from Nirma University, Ahmedabad, where I developed a strong foundation in computer science and began my journey into research.

Research Interests

  • Information Retrieval: Developing advanced retrieval systems and evaluation methodologies
  • Large Language Models: LLM evaluation, robustness, and practical applications
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Designing RAG systems for complex, multimodal tasks and applications
  • Automated Evaluation: Building frameworks for scalable, automated assessment and fact extraction across text and multimodal content
